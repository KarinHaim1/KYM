So What Is That MetaTrader?

MetaTrader is a popular trading platform developed by MetaQuotes Software. It's widely used by retail forex traders due to its user-friendly interface, comprehensive charting tools, automated trading capabilities, and flexibility. The platform supports trading in various financial markets, including forex, commodities, indices, and cryptocurrencies. MetaTrader comes in two main versions: MetaTrader 4 (MT4) and MetaTrader 5 (MT5), each with its own features and functionalities.





MetaTrader 4, released in 2005, is renowned for its robust performance and extensive features that cater primarily to forex traders. Here are the key aspects of MT4:

User Interface Charting Tools Technical Analysis Automated Trading Order Types
Over 30 built-in technical indicators and 24 graphic objects to aid in market analysis. Supports Expert Advisors (EAs), which are automated trading scripts that can execute trades based on predefined criteria. Provides various order types including market orders, pending orders, stop orders, and trailing stops. High-level security protocols ensuring safe trading and data encryption. A large community of traders and developers, offering extensive support, forums, and shared resources.

MetaTrader 5, released in 2010, builds upon the success of MT4 with additional features and enhancements aimed at providing a more comprehensive trading experience. Here's what distinguishes MT5:

Broader Market Coverage Advanced Charting Tools Technical and Fundamental Analysis Order Types and Execution Market Depth
Supports trading in a wider range of financial instruments, including stocks, futures, and options, in addition to forex. Offers more timeframes (21 total) and additional chart types, providing greater flexibility for technical analysis. Includes more built-in technical indicators (38) and graphic objects (44), as well as an economic calendar and fundamental analysis tools. Provides more order types and advanced order execution modes, enhancing trading precision and flexibility. Displays the full market depth, offering greater insight into market liquidity and trading volumes.
